1. Target User Segments
Segment A: Active Retail Crypto Traders (Spot & Perp Traders)
Why them: They trade frequently, already pay for tools (TradingView, Telegram signal groups, bot
subscriptions), and are the fastest segment to convert into a paying or highly engaged early user becausetheproduct maps directly onto something they do every day. Pain point AgentVault solves: Retail traders lose money and time to emotional decisions, missed entries/exits, and juggling multiple charts and news feeds. An AI trading assistant that can watch the market, flag setups, andexecute or suggest trades removes the manual monitoring burden and reduces impulsive mistakes. Where to find them: Crypto Twitter/X (trader threads, #cryptotwitter), Telegram trading and signal groups, Discord servers for exchanges like Binance/Bybit communities, r/CryptoCurrency and r/algotrading, andtradingfocused YouTube/TikTok comment sections. Segment B: DeFi Power Users & Small DAO Treasury Managers
Why them: They manage larger, multi-asset portfolios (personal or DAO treasury funds), are highly Web3-native,and have real incentive to automate monitoring since they are responsible for other people's money or asharedtreasury — making them vocal advocates if the tool works. Pain point AgentVault solves: Treasury managers manually track positions across multiple wallets, protocols, and chains, and often react late to market moves or liquidation risk. AgentVault can act as an always-onco-pilotthat monitors positions and surfaces risk/opportunity signals in real time, saving hours of manual tracking. Where to find them: DAO governance forums (Snapshot, Discourse boards of major DAOs), DeFi-focusedDiscord servers, Twitter/X accounts of treasury multisig signers, and communities like Bankless, DeFi Llama'sDiscord, and Gitcoin/DAO ops groups.
